## About Me

I am a PhD Student in Bioengineering at UC Berkeley advised by Jay Keasling and Patrick Shih. My research focuses on developing molecular technologies and algorithms to model the evolutionary dynamics of gene regulatory systems. Previously, I studied Electrical Engineering & Computer Science, where I worked on multi-agent systems, and Molecular & Cell Biology, where I worked on biological feedback control.

Alongside research, I care about teaching and outreach. I've designed and taught machine learning and statistics courses through UCSF's AI4ALL program, created a Spanish-language course on ML-driven protein design at the Universidad de Guanajuato. I have also served as a graduate student instructor, teaching "Introduction to Biomedicine for Engineers", and as an undergraduate student instructor, teaching "Biophysical Chemistry", at UC Berkeley.

I am grateful to have my work supported by the H2H8 and NSF Graduate Research Fellowships.

## Research Interests

- **Machine learning for biological design:** active learning and model-guided design of proteins and gene-regulatory elements.
- **Evolutionary modeling of gene regulation:** learning the dynamics that shape transcriptional control across evolutionary space.
- **High-throughput & self-driving labs:** closing the loop between large-scale experiments and predictive models.
